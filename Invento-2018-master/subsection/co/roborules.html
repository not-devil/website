Assemble your nuts and bolts and fix your tires, put the wires and motors in their place. Think your robot can overcome any obstacle-big or small in the least of time. If so get it on the track and let the game begin. And bear in mind maximizing RPM doesn't always do the trick though rising pulse rate might.
They're on their own between the start and finish lines - there are no pit stops - and the exact course will remain a secret until the morning of the competition.
You have to maneuver your robot through a series of obstacles that could be wired or wireless.

General Rules-:

  1. 1) The maximum number of members allowed per team is 4.
  2. 2) All students with a valid identity card of their respective educational institutes are eligible to participate in this event.
  3. 3) All the participating teams are requested to be at the venue on time.
  4. 4) The judges decision regarding scoring and judgment will be final
  5. 5) Per team can participate once’s on a race.

Machine Specification:-

  1. 1) The machine should fit within a box of dimensions (25x25x25, LxBxH) cm.
    2) The Machine should not weigh more than 5 KG.
    3) Both wireless and wired machines are allowed.
    4) Use of readymade toy is not allowed.
    5) Chemicals, compressed gases and rockets, fuels, combustion power methods, etc. are strictly prohibited. However commercial batteries like dry cells and lead acid are allowed to use.

    Power Supply: Participants will be allowed to use maximum on board 15V DC power. Use of heavy equipment like compressors is banned.

Safety: The machines you build will be first checked for size and safety. The machine should not cause electrical power breakdowns. The robots must not contain any combustible, corrosive, or otherwise dangerous materials. No explosive, compressive or expansive, either internal or external is permitted. Any robot whose strategy or operation is considered dangerous can be disqualified by the judges at their discretion.

Timelimit: The time limit will be declared by the Team ROBOTRONiX on the spot.


Game Field:The width of the track is 40 cm. The entire game field is divided into no. of sub zones. Each zone contains various hurdles which are as follows: Oil passage, Marbles, Sand, Rollers slope, Slipper slope, Switch, Net bridge etc.
There can be another 2 to 3 surprise hurdles on the field. Participants have to start the game from starting zone and reach to finish line in minimum possible time.

Rules/Foul/Penalties:-

1)Robot is required to complete the given track in minimum time.

2) The performance of the robot will be judged on the basis of the time it takes to complete the arena & also type of hurdle completed.

3) Robot should touch all check point and return back to the start/finish line to finish the race.

4) Maximum of 2 chances will be given to each team either to place robot back on arena or ignoring check point.

5) Only 2 hand touch and 1skeep Each hand touch 10 second penalities and skeep 20 second penalities

6) The robots are not allowed to leave part (s) on any part of the arena. In case some part of the machine falls on the arena, the penalty (including disqualification) will be based on judges’ discretion.

7) Machines damaging any part of the arena will be disqualified. Damaging does not include unintentional scratches on the any surface by the machine. Once again judge’s discretion will be used.

8) During game the participants are not allowed to go inside the game field.

9) Decision taken by the judges would be final and would not be challenged

RoundSystem:The first round will be the elimination round. In this the participants will have to finish 1 to 2 sub zones (containing hurdle)
After the elimination round the difficulty level will be increased in consequent rounds.
